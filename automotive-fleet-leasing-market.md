<p><p>Automotive fleet leasing market is divided into two main types: open ended and close ended. In an open ended lease, the lessee is responsible for the residual value of the vehicle at the end of the lease term, which is determined by the actual market value. On the other hand, in a closed ended lease, the residual value is predetermined at the beginning of the lease, providing more certainty for the lessee. Both types of leases have their own advantages and disadvantages, depending on the specific needs and preferences of the fleet owner.</p></p>
