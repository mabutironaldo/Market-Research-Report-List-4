<p><p>Helical gears are classified into left-hand and right-hand markets based on the direction of the helix angle. Left-hand helical gears have teeth that are angled to the left, while right-hand helical gears have teeth that are angled to the right. Each type of gear is designed to mesh with a specific partner gear, ensuring smooth and efficient power transmission. Left-hand and right-hand helical gears are commonly used in a variety of applications, including automotive, industrial machinery, and power generation.</p></p>
