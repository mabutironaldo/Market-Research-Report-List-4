<p><p>Orthopedic Surgery Navigation Systems help surgeons in precisely placing implants during orthopedic procedures. There are mainly two types of navigational systems used in orthopedic surgeries - Optical Surgical Navigation Systems and Electromagnetic Surgical Navigation Systems. Optical systems use cameras and infrared light to track the position of surgical instruments inside the patient's body. Electromagnetic systems utilize electromagnetic fields to provide real-time tracking of instruments. Additionally, there are other navigation systems in the market that may use different technologies or a combination of optical and electromagnetic tracking methods.</p></p>
