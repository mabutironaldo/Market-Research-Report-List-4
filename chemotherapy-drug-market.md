<p><p>The chemotherapy drug market can be segmented into types such as Antimetabolites, Plant Alkaloids, Alkylating Agents, and Antitumor Antibiotics. Antimetabolites are drugs that interfere with the process of cell division by acting as substitutes for the building blocks of DNA. Plant Alkaloids are derived from natural sources and disrupt cell division. Alkylating Agents work by attaching alkyl groups to DNA strands, preventing cell replication. Antitumor Antibiotics disrupt DNA replication in cancer cells, leading to their destruction.</p></p>
