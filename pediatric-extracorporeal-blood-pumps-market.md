<p><p>Pediatric extracorporeal blood pumps are medical devices used to circulate blood outside the body during procedures like heart surgeries. The market consists of various types of pumps including roller pumps, centrifugal pumps, pulsatile pumps, non-occlusive pumps, and others. Roller pumps use rotating rollers to push blood through tubing, while centrifugal pumps use rotating impellers. Pulsatile pumps mimic the natural pulsing of the heart. Non-occlusive pumps provide continuous blood flow without fully occluding the tubing. Each type has its own advantages and applications in pediatric healthcare.</p></p>
