<p><p>Commercial dough conditioners come in two main types - powders and fluids. Powders are dry ingredients that are added to dough to improve its texture, elasticity, and rise. They are easy to store and have a long shelf life. Fluids, on the other hand, are liquid-based ingredients that can be easily mixed into dough. They offer faster and more consistent results compared to powders. Both types of dough conditioners are commonly used in bakeries and food manufacturing operations to enhance the quality and consistency of baked goods.</p></p>
