<p><p>Shape-memory polymers are a versatile material used in various industries due to their ability to return to their original shape when exposed to certain stimuli. Temperature-induced shape-memory polymers change shape when exposed to heat or cold. Light-induced polymers respond to specific wavelengths of light. Electricity-induced polymers change shape when an electric current is applied. Other types include pH-induced polymers that respond to changes in acidity or alkalinity, and magnetic-induced polymers that change shape in the presence of a magnetic field.</p></p>
