<p><p>Automotive Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) Simulation is a testing method that allows for the real-time simulation of a vehicle's electronic control unit (ECU) in a virtual environment. This technology enables automotive manufacturers to test the performance and functionality of ECUs in a controlled and repeatable manner before physical prototypes are built. This helps to reduce development time and costs, while ensuring the safety and reliability of vehicle systems.</p><p>The Automotive Hardware-in-the-Loop Simulation Market is experiencing significant growth, with a projected CAGR of 5.9% during the forecast period.
