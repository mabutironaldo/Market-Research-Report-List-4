<p><p>Anti-tank guided missiles are divided into three main types based on their method of deployment: shoulder-fired, tripod-fired, and vehicle-fired. Shoulder-fired missiles are portable and can be carried and launched by a single soldier. Tripod-fired missiles require a stable base for launching and are typically used by infantry teams. Vehicle-fired missiles are mounted on vehicles such as tanks or armored vehicles and can be operated remotely. Each type has its own advantages and is suited for different combat scenarios.</p></p>
